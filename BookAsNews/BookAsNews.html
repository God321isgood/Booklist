Debt - The First 5000 Years

by

DAVID GRAEBER

In a stunning of conventional wisdom, Graeber

radically challenges our understanding of debt. He illustrates

how for more than 5000 years long before the invention of coins or bills, there existed debtors and creditors who used

elaborate credit systems to buy and sell goods.

He argues that Madagascar was held to be indebted to

France because France invaded it, reminds us that texts from

Vedic india included God in credit systems and shows how

the dollar changed European society forever in the sixteenth

century.
